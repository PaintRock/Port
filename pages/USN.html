My Favorite Home

For nearly three years, I called Ford Island home.

This aerial view captures it as I remember it - notice there's no bridge connecting to the mainland we could only arrive by boat or air.

Waikiki, which is roughly 2 miles long and about 1/2 mile deep, has the population density of New York City, everyday.

Ford Island was the opposite as it was restricted to government personnel and very few wanted to live there. It was peaceful, and quiet, and we had our own coconut tree right out side the window.
